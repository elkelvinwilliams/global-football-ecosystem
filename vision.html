How it works

From a phone camera to a verified record.

Film

A registered coach or combine official films the match — any modern phone is enough. Footage is hashed at capture, so it can't be altered later.

Certify

The official confirms the fixture, the teams and each player's passport identity. No certification, no data — highlight reels alone prove nothing.

Analyse

Vision tracks every player and the ball, detecting 1,200+ events per match — sprints, duels, passes, shots, saves — within 24 hours.

Benchmark

Metrics land on the player's passport, benchmarked against global age-group peers — so a scout can compare Dakar with Zagreb on equal terms.

What it measures

Position-aware, not one-size-fits-all.

Forwards

The sharp end

Sprint profile and top speed, xG per shot, movement in the final third, pressing triggers, finishing under pressure — the runs that never make a highlight reel.

Midfielders

The engine room

Progressive passes and carries, ball recoveries, press resistance, scanning frequency, build-up zones — the game between the goals, measured.

Defenders

The back line

Duels ground and aerial, line height, recovery pace, line-breaking distribution, defensive positioning against the run of play.

Goalkeepers

The last word

Save rate by zone, reaction time, sweeping range, distribution accuracy under pressure, command of the area at set pieces.

Integrity

Data a career can stand on.

Tamper-evident

Sealed at capture

Footage is fingerprinted the moment it's recorded. If a frame changes between the pitch and the passport, certification fails.

Human in the loop

Officials sign off

AI measures; registered people vouch. Every certified match carries the name of the official who confirmed it — accountability travels with the data.

Built for minors

Analysis, not surveillance

Vision measures football actions on the pitch, nothing else. Guardians see everything collected, and the player owns the record for life.
